ü§î The Redundancy Question

Central Question

Now that Homepage does the "What I Do + CTAs" job, what unique value should About and Now provide?

Information Architecture Options

Option A: Distinct Purposes Model

  • Homepage: Action gateway (what's available now)
  • About: Credibility & philosophy (why trust me, what I believe)
  • Now: Current explorations (what I'm thinking about, hybrid status/action)
  • Projects: Portfolio (what I've made)
  • Writing: Ideas (what I'm exploring in depth)

Option B: Consolidation Model

  • Homepage: Action gateway + brief bio
  • About: Deep dive (full story, philosophy, credentials in one place)
  • Remove Now page? Fold into About or Homepage
  • Projects: Portfolio with current work highlighted
  • Writing: Ideas & resources

Option C: Audience-Based Model

  • Homepage: Action gateway for all visitors
  • About: For clients/collaborators (credentials, approach, trust-building)
  • Now: For peers/friends (what you're exploring, personal interests)
  • Projects: For portfolio browsers (extended CV)
  • Writing: For idea-seekers (depth on topics you care about)

The mockups below explore these different models. Click through the options to see what resonates.

The Stone Stairs

I enjoy function and public utility. The weave between practical and beauty. A stone staircase with slowly rounded edges, curved by generations of feet stepping into a library. This benefit for the public good and effort across time encapsulates my driving force.

How This Shows Up in Practice

In therapeutic work: Sustained attention to patterns. Helping people notice what lives in the frame and what breathes just outside it. Not rushing transformation, but trusting what emerges through continuity.

In creative work: Working emergently within constraints. The frame isn't a limitation but a focusing device. What wants to emerge within these specific boundaries, at this specific time?

In contemplative practice: Showing up daily to group meditation. Building capacity through relational practice. The skill develops not through intensity but through consistency.
